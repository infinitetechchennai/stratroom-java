How the risk score is calculated
Every factor (SWOT or PESTLE) is rated on two independent axes: Impact (the magnitude of effect on the organisation if the factor materialises) and Likelihood (the probability it will materialise within the planning horizon). The Risk Score = Impact × Likelihood, ranging from 1 (trivial) to 25 (critical). Each factor is then automatically plotted in the cell where those two scores intersect. Zones:
Critical (20–25) High (12–19) Moderate (6–11) Low (1–5)
